About This

Savkosii
2022-04-12

I initially set up this blog in order to share (or simply record) what I’d learnt on the course of my Web Security learning, which started after I happened to partake the MoeCTF, a Cyber Security game held by XDSEC and oriented towards freshman in our school. Then I found tackling CTF problems not only intersting, but also served me quite well in the aspect of Computer Science learning. e.g., I’d learnt linux and gained some OS basis for the first time, and also found myself exposed to some misc handy concepts like hash, regular expression, http, git and etc. Most importantly, I’d learnt how to ask questions and use searching enginees properly to increase my working efficiency. (I think it quite necessary to say farewell to Baidu and CSDN for any IT practitioners in China).

Near the end of the last semester, I occasionally (maybe just affected by insights from someone’s blogs) started to think about what attitude I actually held towards Web Security. Should that be my future career? Almost at the very beginning I’d found that the methods of learning Web Security are quite limited. The theories basis are always not highlighted, at least in the context of CTF. It seems that for a freshman in this field, what they can do to improve their skills is to gather fragmented knowledges that scatter everywhere on the Internet. This way of learning was actually frustrating for me. And apparently, Web Security is not just about CTF. It is a subject that contains more, and also a very indepentent field that requires devotion and will inevitably end up taking up one’s most of the time, which wasn’t what I previously expected. In fact I didn’t think it worthy to study it in price of learning other interesting fields like system programming. I was just young enough (?) and I felt like exploring whatever I had interest in. Since that I didn’t have the capacity to juggle both, why not take only one?

As a consequence, this blog might no longer post any notes or insights relevant to Web Security. Instead, I will just write some trivial personal thoughts like this post, as I think it a better platform where I can unservedly express what I actually want to express with nothing to be shy of, versus other platforms like Moments in Wechat. And anything that I get interested in relevant to CS might be included as well.
